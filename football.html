QATAR 2022: SPORTSWASHING AT PLANETARY SCALE

The 2022 FIFA World Cup was awarded to Qatar in 2010, by a vote in which at least six of the 22 FIFA executive committee members who voted have since been charged with corruption. Qatar, a peninsula of 3 million people with summer temperatures of 50°C, was selected over Australia, the United States, Japan, and South Korea. The decision was announced by Sepp Blatter, who was later banned from football for 8 years.

The construction of eight World Cup stadiums required the labor of an estimated 1.5 million migrant workers, predominantly from India, Nepal, Bangladesh, and Pakistan, working under a kafala sponsorship system that human rights organizations have characterized as conditions of labor bondage. The Guardian documented over 6,500 worker deaths over the construction decade. FIFA never commissioned an independent investigation.

"Football has become the last truly global soft power commodity — and the Gulf states understand this far better than democracies do."

— Former EU sports policy advisor, off-record briefing, Brussels 2023

THE PREMIER LEAGUE IS A SOVEREIGN WEALTH OPERATION

As of 2024, four of the twenty Premier League clubs are majority-owned by entities directly connected to Middle Eastern or Asian sovereign wealth funds: Manchester City (UAE), Newcastle United (Saudi Arabia), Paris Saint-Germain's competitor Chelsea (Russian oligarch, replaced by American consortium with Gulf links), and several others with state-adjacent ownership. This is not sports investment. It is geopolitical adjacency purchasing — buying access to the world's most watched media property to launder national brand reputation.

Manchester City's Financial Fair Play charges revealed a pattern of financial engineering so sophisticated that it required the club to produce over 12,000 documents in defense. The Premier League's own independent hearing has dragged for three-plus years — a timeline that, critics note, would have concluded in weeks if the club were relegated to League One.

// Theory Alpha

THE BCCI SOVEREIGNTY PROBLEM

The Board of Control for Cricket in India is officially a private trust — which means it is exempt from the Right to Information Act, exempt from government audit, and accountable to no elected authority. Yet it controls a global television rights deal worth ₹48,390 crore, manages franchise fees totaling hundreds of thousands of crores, and determines the career trajectories of the world's highest-paid cricketers.

The BCCI president and secretary are elected — but by state cricket associations, not by fans, players, or the public. State associations are controlled by politicians from every major party. The result is a cartel structure where electoral competitors in government become business partners in cricket governance. No conflict of interest has ever been officially declared.

"Cricket is not a sport in India. It is the largest unregulated financial exchange in Asia."

— Former SEBI consultant, anonymous briefing, 2019
// Franchise Ownership Network

WHO REALLY OWNS YOUR TEAM?

IPL franchise ownership is a labyrinth of holding companies, SPVs, and subsidiary structures designed to obscure the ultimate beneficial owner. The franchise is technically owned by an entity which is owned by a trust which is governed by a foundation registered in a tax haven. Trace any franchise to its true owner and you arrive at either a political family, a real estate empire, or an entity with undisclosed sovereign wealth connections.

// Theory Beta

THE SPOT-FIXING ICEBERG

The 2013 spot-fixing scandal resulted in three players being banned and five bookies arrested. The Supreme Court of India's Lodha Committee confirmed widespread corruption. Zero franchise owners were investigated. Zero administrators were charged. The inquiry was handed to the BCCI itself — the institution whose financial structure enabled the corruption — to conduct.

Intelligence sources have repeatedly briefed that live in-play betting on IPL matches generates between $8–12 billion annually through illegal markets alone. At that scale, the incentive to control match outcomes is mathematical, not speculative. Every investigation has concluded at the player level. Not one has followed the money upward.

// Theory Gamma

THE BROADCAST-POLITICS NEXUS

The Star India and Jio Cinema media rights deal didn't just sell cricket — it locked the content consumption habits of 500 million Indians into specific platforms. Those platforms are owned by entities with deep political relationships. When you combine content captivity with algorithmic targeting capabilities, you have the most powerful political propaganda infrastructure in democratic history, camouflaged as entertainment.

// Theory Alpha

PATENTS DON'T PROTECT INNOVATION — THEY PROHIBIT IT

When a pharmaceutical company patents a drug, it doesn't just protect its investment in developing that drug. It creates a 20-year legal monopoly that prohibits any other entity — university, NGO, government lab — from producing the same compound, regardless of urgency or humanitarian need. The TRIPS Agreement, negotiated at the WTO, extended this system globally, overriding local health laws in 164 countries.

The consequence: a generic version of a patented cancer drug that costs $3 to produce sells for $15,000 per course in the United States because no alternative manufacturer is legally permitted to exist. When South Africa attempted to manufacture generic AIDS drugs during its epidemic in 1998, 39 pharmaceutical companies filed suit. The legal battle lasted four years — during which time an estimated 360,000 South Africans died of AIDS-related illness.

"We have no interest in discovering a cure. Cures are bad for business. Managing a chronic condition is the business model."

— Attributed to internal Bain & Company consulting brief, 2019 — widely leaked, never sued over
// The Suppression Timeline

TREATMENTS DELAYED, BURIED, OR BOUGHT OUT

1953
Antineoplastons — Stanislaw Burzynski
Burzynski claimed his peptide treatment showed results in brain tumors. FDA conducted 5 grand jury investigations over 14 years, found no fraud, conducted no clinical trials. The treatment remains unpatentable — no pharmaceutical company can profit from it.
1976
Ivermectin Research — Early Parasite Studies
Merck discovers ivermectin's antiparasitic properties. Wins Nobel. Later research on broader antiviral properties was systematically defunded when patent expiry made it commercially irrelevant.
1990s
SSRIs Replace Psychotherapy
Pharmaceutical industry successfully lobbied to shift depression treatment protocols from psychotherapy (one-time cost) to SSRIs (lifetime prescription). Cognitive behavioral therapy shows equivalent long-term outcomes at lower cost. It is systematically underinsured.
2010
Alzheimer's Drug Pipeline Collapses
After 30+ years and $40B+ in failed drug trials, the amyloid hypothesis remains pharmaceutical consensus. Researchers who proposed alternative mechanisms report difficulty receiving funding. The failing hypothesis is commercially embedded.
2023
GLP-1 Obesity Drugs
Highly effective. Patented. $1,000/month. The same mechanism has been achievable through fasting protocols documented for decades. You cannot patent fasting.
// Theory Beta

THE FDA REVOLVING DOOR

Between 2006 and 2019, 57% of all senior FDA officials who left government employment moved directly into positions at pharmaceutical companies they had previously regulated. This is not a conflict of interest. It is the structure of interest — the captured regulator is the model, not the exception.

01
Opioid Crisis Was a Product Launch
Purdue Pharma's internal documents, released in bankruptcy proceedings, show executives knew OxyContin was highly addictive in 1996 and chose to market it as "less addictive" anyway. 500,000 Americans died. The Sackler family paid $6B in settlements while keeping $11B in assets.
02
Clinical Trial Publication Bias
Pharmaceutical companies are not required to publish negative clinical trial results. Studies showing a drug doesn't work are quietly shelved. Only positive results reach prescribing doctors. This is legal. It is also the scientific fraud that built the antidepressant market.
03
Disease Mongering
The pharmaceutical industry funds awareness campaigns for conditions it then treats. Social anxiety disorder, ADHD, restless leg syndrome, and 40+ other diagnoses were expanded or created through industry-funded psychiatrist panels. DSM revisions correlate directly with new drug approvals.
04
The Vitamin D Suppression Story
Decades of research link severe vitamin D deficiency to cancer, autoimmune disease, and respiratory illness. Correcting deficiency costs pennies per day and cannot be patented. Funding for this research has been systematically deficient. Insurance coverage for testing is minimal.
